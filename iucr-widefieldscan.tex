\begin{abstract}
State-of-the-art synchrotron-based tomographic microscopy end-stations acquire volumetric data at micrometer level within a few minutes. It is therefore possible to investigate large objects at high resolution using local tomographic reconstruction or extending the field of view in vertical direction by combining the data of several scans.

The present work defines the acquisition protocols by which the field of view can be increased in horizontal direction beyond the field of view of a standard synchrotron radiation based x-ray tomographic microscope.

We show that the lateral field of view can be increased in horizontal direction up to five times to acquire wide field scans of arbitrary samples in an unattended, automatic way based on objective criteria like resolution and contrast of the reconstructed slices  and scanning time into account.
\end{abstract}
